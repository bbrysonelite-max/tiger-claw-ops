# Tiger Bot Flywheel Webpage — Design Brainstorm

## Context
An interactive webpage presenting the Tiger Bot onboarding system (customer interview + ICP development) and the complete flywheel (Discovery → First Contact → Nurturing → Conversion → Retention) for gig economy workers. Tiger Bot brand uses orange as its primary color. The audience is non-technical gig economy workers who need to understand how the system works.

## Idea 1: "War Room Command Center"

**Design Movement:** Cyberpunk-industrial dashboard aesthetic, inspired by military command centers and sci-fi HUDs.

**Core Principles:**
1. Information density with clarity — every pixel earns its place
2. Dark environments with high-contrast accent lighting
3. Data feels alive, like a real-time operations center
4. Aggressive, forward-leaning energy that matches the Tiger Bot brand

**Color Philosophy:** Near-black background (#0A0A0F) with Tiger orange (#F97316) as the primary accent, electric amber (#FBBF24) as secondary, and cool slate (#64748B) for tertiary information. The orange glows against the dark — it feels like a tiger's eyes in the dark. Danger and power.

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-bleed dark canvas with floating panels and cards arranged in an asymmetric command-center grid. The flywheel is a large, animated circular diagram at the center of the page. Sections are "stations" you scroll through, each with its own panel-like treatment.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Glowing orange border-lines and subtle scan-line effects on panels
2. A rotating, interactive flywheel diagram with glowing nodes at each stage
3. Monospace data readouts for metrics and statistics

**Interaction Philosophy:** Hovering over flywheel stages reveals detailed information panels that slide in from the side. Clicking stages drills down. Everything feels like activating a system.

**Animation:** Panels fade in with a slight upward drift and opacity change. The flywheel rotates slowly on idle. Stage transitions use a quick horizontal slide. Numbers count up when they enter the viewport.

**Typography System:** "Space Grotesk" for headings (bold, technical feel), "Inter" for body text at 400/500 weight. Monospace "JetBrains Mono" for data labels and metrics.

## Idea 2: "Blueprint Architect"

**Design Movement:** Technical blueprint / architectural drawing aesthetic — clean, precise, and authoritative. Inspired by engineering schematics and Dieter Rams' design principles.

**Core Principles:**
1. Precision and structure communicate competence
2. The system is the product — show the engineering behind it
3. White space is a structural element, not emptiness
4. Every line and connection has meaning

**Color Philosophy:** Warm off-white background (#FAFAF8) with deep charcoal (#1C1917) for text. Tiger orange (#EA580C) is used sparingly as the "highlight ink" — like an engineer's red pen marking the critical path. A muted stone (#A8A29E) for secondary lines and grid elements. The restraint makes the orange feel powerful when it appears.

**Layout Paradigm:** A long-scroll, left-anchored layout with a persistent vertical progress line running down the left margin. Content sections are offset to the right. The flywheel is drawn as a technical schematic with labeled connection lines. Interview sections use form-like card layouts that feel like official documents.

**Signature Elements:**
1. A thin vertical progress line with orange dots marking each section
2. Dashed connection lines between flywheel stages, like a circuit diagram
3. Section labels styled as blueprint annotations (small caps, offset from content)

**Interaction Philosophy:** Scrolling advances through the blueprint. Hovering over connection lines highlights the data flow between stages. The experience is methodical and sequential — you understand the system by walking through it.

**Animation:** Minimal and purposeful. Lines draw themselves as you scroll into view. Cards fade in with a slight 8px upward shift. The flywheel diagram assembles itself piece by piece as you reach it.

**Typography System:** "DM Sans" for headings (clean, geometric authority), "Source Serif 4" for body text (readable, trustworthy). Small caps "DM Sans" for labels and annotations.

## Idea 3: "Predator's Path"

**Design Movement:** Bold editorial / sports-brand aesthetic — think Nike campaign meets Bloomberg terminal. High energy, high contrast, unapologetic confidence.

**Core Principles:**
1. Bold typography dominates — the message hits you before the design does
2. Diagonal energy and forward motion in every layout choice
3. Orange is not an accent — it IS the brand, used at full volume
4. The system feels like a competitive advantage, not a tool

**Color Philosophy:** Deep black (#09090B) as the primary background with full-intensity Tiger orange (#F97316) as the dominant brand color used in large blocks and gradients. Pure white (#FFFFFF) for text on dark. A warm dark gray (#27272A) for card surfaces. The palette is binary — black and orange — with no soft middle ground. It's aggressive by design.

**Layout Paradigm:** Stacked full-width sections that alternate between dark and orange backgrounds. Content uses a wide two-column or three-column grid with oversized typography breaking the grid intentionally. The flywheel is presented as a large horizontal timeline/journey with bold stage markers. Diagonal clip-paths create sharp transitions between sections.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Oversized display type (80px+) for section headers, sometimes cropped by the viewport edge
2. Diagonal dividers between sections using CSS clip-path
3. Bold orange highlight blocks behind key phrases, like a highlighter pen

**Interaction Philosophy:** Scrolling is a journey — each section hits with impact. Hover states are immediate and bold (color inversions, scale bumps). The page feels like it's pushing you forward toward action.

**Animation:** Sections enter with a fast, confident slide from the left or bottom. Text reveals with a clip animation (words appear as if being typed or stamped). The flywheel stages animate in sequence with a staggered delay. Counters snap to their final number quickly.

**Typography System:** "Bebas Neue" for display headings (condensed, loud, commanding), "Outfit" for body and UI text (modern, clean, highly readable). The contrast between the condensed display and the open body font creates visual tension and hierarchy.

## Selected Approach: Idea 3 — "Predator's Path"

This approach best matches the Tiger Bot brand identity — aggressive, confident, and action-oriented. The bold editorial style with oversized typography and the black/orange palette communicates power and competitive advantage, which is exactly what gig economy workers need to feel when they see this system. The diagonal energy and forward motion reinforce the flywheel concept of momentum building over time.
